Chapter Summaries

  • Act 1 Scene 1
  • The play begins outside on a street in Venice, Italy
  • Two characters, Roderigo and Iago, are arguing
  • Roderigo is upset with Iago because he has been paying him to help him court a beautiful women Desdemona however she married another man, Othello, instead
  • Roderigo believes that Iago helped this happen, however Iago assures him that he hates Othello
  • Iago explains that he is upset that Othello, a general, did not choose him to lead his army into battle, but instead chose another less experienced man, therefore Iago only pretends to be loyal to Othello
  • Iago plots with Roderigo of a way to spoil some of Othello's great pleasure with his new marriage by causing a rift between him and Desdemona's family
  • The two decide to travel to the house of Desdemona's parents where they call out to her father Brabanzio, a senator, claiming they have been robbed
  • Brabanzio first refuses to listen to them as he believes it is another plot for Roderigo to speak with Desdemona, however Iago convinces him otherwise
  • Iago claims that Othello and Desdemona are having sex which greatly upsets Brabanzio and causes him to go search for his daughter
  • Iago takes the opportunity to leave and attend to Othello after his accusations
  • Brabanzio gathers his men before heading out to search for Othello, his men follow the lead of Roderigo
  • Act 1 Scene 2
  • Iago arrives at Othello's home and warns him of Brabanzio and his men
  • Iago claims that Brabanzio is coming to force Othello to divorce Desdemona
  • While they are speaking Othello sees a group of men approaching, Iago, believing that it is Brabanzio's men, instructs Othello to seek shelter inside
  • Othello refuses to retreat and stands his ground, the two men are surprised however that the party is in fact led by Cassio and officers of the Venetian court
  • Their orders are to inform Othello that he must meet with the duke of Venice over a matter concerning Cyprus, a nearby island
  • Cassio and his men prepare to leave but not before Iago begins to mention Othello's new marriage, however this is interrupted by the arrival of Brabanzio, his men, and Roderigo
  • A skirmish ensues between Othello's men and Brabanzio's, however Othello manages to stop it by speaking to both sides
  • Brabanzio decides that instead of having a battle he will bring his case against Othello to the duke
  • Act 1 Scene 3
  • The play shifts to the duke's court
  • The duke and his men are discussing the state of Cyprus
  • It appears as if a group of Turkish invaders who initially appeared headed to attack Cyprus have changed their course to the island of Rhodes
  • The men believe this is an attempt by the Turks to confuse the Venetians as the logical place to attack would be Cyprus as it is much more valuable
  • The meeting however is suddenly interrupted by Brabanzio, Othello, Cassio, Iago, and Roderigo
  • Brabanzio demands the meeting be stopped at once to address his accusations
  • He claims that his daughter has been bewitched into marrying a man against her will
  • The duke at first begins to believe him, however when he learns that Othello is being accused he begins to alter his beliefs
  • The duke decides to give Othello a chance to defend himself
  • Othello explains that the love between him and Desdemona is mutual
  • He tells of how they met when Othello was invited over to tell of his war stories to Brabanzio
  • Othello adds that Desdemona had overheard his stories and later spoken to him alone, they soon fell in love
  • Desdemona is finally brought in for questioning to resolve the matter
  • Sure enough she sides with Othello saying that she loves him and is loyal to him as her new husband is more important now then her father
  • Brabanzio is shocked, yet he resigns himself to what he has just learned and lets the court resume its work
  • The court then decides that Othello will travel to Cyprus with his army to defend the Venetians
  • Othello willingly obliges, however their are issues as to what Desdemona will do while he is gone
  • After debating it is decided that she will travel with him
  • Everyone on stage leaves except for Roderigo and Iago
  • The two argue some more as Roderigo feels hopeless about ever winning Desdemona's heart
  • Iago explains that this is all part of the plan and urges Roderigo to relax
  • Iago adds that the two must travel to Cyprus so it can all be worked out
  • Roderigo's spirits are slightly lifted and he walks off stage leaving Iago behind where he gives a soliloquy
  • Iago explains that he hates Othello and plans to break up the marriage between him and Desdemona by making everyone believe that Cassio has slept with Desdemona
  • He adds that he has no intentions of helping Roderigo win Desdemona's hand, and that his ultimate goal is to bring Othello to his death
  • Act 2 Scene 1
  • The play shifts to Cyprus where Montano, the governor, watches the seas with two other men
  • They look out into a tempest, or large storm, and discuss the Turkish fleet
  • They all three agree that their is no way the fleet could have survived the storm and hope for the best for the island
  • Meanwhile Cassio has witnessed a large number of Turkish ships being destroyed by the storm while Cassio traveled by ship from Venice
  • Cassio is unaware of whether Othello's ship survived the storm
  • Hope suddenly rises for Othello on the island of Cyprus when their are reports of approaching ship, however it turns out to be only Iago, Emilia (Iago's wife), Desdemona, and Roderigo
  • Desdemona inquires about the fate of her husband yet he has still not arrived
  • Soon after they are interrupted by reports that a third ship has arrived, believed to be Othello's
  • Before any more news is learned Cassio and Desdemona tease Emilia about being too talkative
  • However the playful banter soon turns to more as Iago butts in and begins to speak of women as being less important than men
  • This is interrupted however by Cassio pulling Desdemona aside by grabbing her hand
  • The two discuss the arrival of Othello's ship
  • Iago notices the gesture of Cassio grabbing Desdemona by the hand and decides to use what he sees to get Cassio in trouble. He hopes Cassio will be demoted from lieutenant
  • Othello eventually joins the scene and has a loving embrace with Desdemona
  • He then goes on to thank all the citizens of Cyprus for their hospitality
  • Othello exclaims that they will all celebrate the Turkish fleets demise in the storm by feasting in the castle, he orders Iago not to join but instead attend to unloading the ship
  • Roderigo joins Iago in unloading the ship and the two discuss Desdemona some more
  • Iago explains that soon enough she will become tired with Othello and desire another man
  • Iago believes that her first choice of another man will be Cassio however. He cites the gesture of him holding her hand as proof
  • Roderigo dismisses this, yet Iago insists that in order to prevent it from happening he must start a quarrel with Cassio that evening
  • Iago again is left alone on stage and provides another soliloquy
  • This time he explains that he hopes to break up the marriage because he lusts for Desdemona, partially because he believes that Othello slept with his wife Emilia
  • Iago adds that even if he cannot win over Desdemona the accusations by Roderigo towards Cassio about sleeping with Desdemona will surely cause Othello madness
  • Act 2 Scene 2
  • A herald appears on stage announcing that Othello is planning a feast for the evening to celebrate the victory and Othello's marriage to Desdemona
  • Act 2 Scene 3
  • While Othello's feast goes on Cassio is left to tend guard
  • During the feast Othello and Desdemona leave to consummate the marriage
  • Meanwhile Iago joins Cassio on guard
  • Iago makes accusations about Desdemona being a temptress however Cassio refuses to agree
  • Iago convinces Cassio to take a drink and relax, he then sends him off to bring back others to enjoy the evening with them
  • While Cassio is gone Iago tells the audience of his plan
  • Once Cassio is drunk, he will convince him to commit a shameful act
  • The group is joined by a drunk Montano and Roderigo who get Cassio even more riled up
  • Cassio is so drunk that he is running on and off stage
  • Meanwhile Montano makes comments about his skills as a soldier, and adds that he believes it a shame that he has such a bad drinking problem
  • Eventually Cassio begins to get out of hand, even chasing Roderigo around the stage
  • Montano steps in to stop Cassio, however Cassio begins to fight back even stabbing Montano
  • An alarm is quickly sounded and Othello is alerted of the incident
  • Othello demands to know what happened and Iago and Cassio have conflicting views on how the struggle began
  • Montano who is not injured too severely explains that Iago should tell his story as he is more trustworthy
  • Iago begins to tell of what occurred however softening up on the accusations of Cassio, this is all part of his plan however
  • Much to Iago's liking, Othello believes that Iago is going light on Cassio out of respect
  • Othello resolves to send Cassio away and dismiss him from his service
  • During all the commotion Desdemona is awoken and goes to see what has occurred
  • Othello assures her that everything is under control, he sends her back to sleep and goes to attend to Montano's wound
  • Cassio and Iago are left behind on stage
  • Cassio laments at his mistakes and the problems facing him
  • Iago suggests that he appeal to Desdemona for leniency and due to her relationship with Othello Cassio might regain his role as lieutenant
  • Iago is then left alone again, he tells the audience that when he does his most evil is actually when he pretends to do good
  • He adds that he will use Cassio's appeals to Desdemona as evidence of a false affair to convince Othello that Desdemona is unfaithful
  • Roderigo enters and again complains about Iago's failure to help him court Desdemona
  • Iago assures him that this is al part of the plan and to be patient
  • Iago sends Roderigo away and then tells the rest of his plot to the audience
  • Iago plans to have Emilia speak with Desdemona on behalf of Cassio therefore allowing Cassio the opportunity to approach Desdemona
  • After this Iago will begin to mount false evidence of an affair between the two
  • Act 3 Scene 1
  • Cassio begins to work to get back on Othello's good side
  • He sends musicians to Othello's window to play for Othello, however Othello is annoyed and sends them away
  • Cassio then tries going to Emilia in hopes she can give him an opportunity to speak to Desdemona
  • Iago then enters and explains that he will send for Emilia so that she can help Cassio
  • He adds that he will try to get Othello away from Desdemona for a time so that Cassio and Desdemona can speak alone
  • Emilia finally enters and speaks with Cassio, she explains that she has set up a chance for Desdemona to meet him
  • Cassio is left alone to wait for the meeting
  • Act 3 Scene 2
  • The scene shifts to Iago, Othello, and an unknown man taking a walk together
  • Othello gives Iago a task of delivering some letters
  • Othello then heads off to observe the towns fortification
  • Act 3 Scene 3
  • The scene opens as Desdemona, Cassio, and Emilia enter the stage in conversation
  • Desdemona tells Cassio that she will do all she can to help plead his case to Othello
  • Meanwhile Othello and Iago enter the stage, out of fear of hurting his favor with Othello even worse, Cassio exits the stage
  • Othello does not quite see Cassio, however Iago assures him that it was Cassio leaving, and that his actions make him appear to be a guilty man
  • Desdemona takes her chance and pleads Cassio's case however Othello is very short with her
  • Othello says he will speak with Cassio, however he does not promise anything nor will he give a meeting time to Desdemona
  • Othello then asks for some peace and quiet
  • Othello however is not left alone and instead Iago begins to push him with allegations of Desdemona's infidelity
  • At first Othello does not want to believe any of this but Iago's testimony begins to make him think it could be true
  • Iago suggests that Othello watch Desdemona closely over the next few days
  • Iago then tells Othello that it is probably not true and that he should actually just leave it alone, however the idea has already been planted in Othello's mind and he begins to think his wife is gone for sure
  • Iago leaves and Desdemona and Emilia enter
  • Desdemona tells her husband that he has been summoned for dinner, however he replies saying he would rather not attend as he has a headache
  • Desdemona tries to comfort him by pressing her handkerchief to his head, however he says that this will not help and it falls to the ground
  • The two walk off the stage leaving Emilia. She notices the kerchief and remarks that her husband, Iago, has been trying to get his hands on the kerchief for quite some time, she picks it up and when Iago enters the stage she gives it to him causing him to become ecstatic
  • Iago is then joined by Othello who demands hard evidence of an affair between Cassio and his wife as he is being tormented by the thought
  • Iago tells him that once while sharing a bed with Cassio that Cassio called out for Desdemona in his sleep, he also says that Cassio has been using Desdemona's handkerchief
  • Othello vows revenge on his wife and Cassio and Iago agrees to help him exact it
  • Othello then promotes Iago to lieutenant
  • Act 3 Scene 4
  • Desdemona is on the stage speaking with a messenger, she sends the messenger to inform Cassio that she has spoken with Othello on his behalf
  • Desdemona is then met by Othello who begins his accusations of her unfaithfulness
  • He then proceeds to ask her for her handkerchief explaining its origin
  • It had been given to his mother by an Egyptian sorceress who had said it would make her desirable and keep her father loyal
  • The kerchief was then given to Othello to give to his wife with the explanation that as long as she has it she will prove to be faithful, however if she is to lose it her unfaithfulness would be proven
  • Othello demands to see it however Desdemona cannot produce it as she claims that it is not on her
  • Othello leaves the stage outraged leaving Emilia and Desdemona behind
  • Emilia comforts Desdemona by explaining that men are just fickle, and that perhaps it is something else that is bothering Othello
  • Cassio then enters to speak with Desdemona about Othello
  • Desdemona tells him that it is not a good time as Othello is a bit enraged
  • Iago who was also on stage sets off to comfort Iago, Desdemona meanwhile decides that she too should go see Othello and try to calm him down
  • Cassio is left alone until he is joined by Bianca, a prostitute
  • Bianca is upset with Cassio as he has not visited her as often as she would like
  • Cassio promises to change this and asks her for a favor, he wants her to make a copy of Desdemona's kerchief
  • Bianca is reluctant at first yet is eventually convinced, the two part ways but decide to meet later that night
  • Act 4 Scene 1
  • Othello and Iago are on stage arguing about Othello's situation
  • Iago continues to convince Othello that Desdemona is sleeping with Cassio, he even goes as far as to say that Cassio has even admitted to it
  • Othello is so upset by this he collapses in a haze
  • Cassio then enters the stage and speaks with Iago, Iago explains that Othello has fallen in a fit of epilepsy
  • Iago sends Cassio away for a minute when Othello begins to come out of his trance
  • Iago tells Othello that Cassio has stopped by and that he will try to get Cassio to admit sleeping with Desdemona
  • Iago instructs Othello to hide so he can witness this
  • While Othello is hiding Cassio and Iago talk, however Iago jokes with him about the prostitute Bianca and not Desdemona
  • Cassio gladly boasts about Bianca's pursuit of him, however from Othello's angle he believes Cassio is speaking about Desdemona
  • Bianca then enters the stage waving around the handkerchief Cassio had given her earlier and complains about it, she demands that Cassio join her for diner that evening
  • Othello waits till Cassio leaves and enters the stage to speak with Iago, the two plot how Othello will murder Cassio
  • Desdemona enters the stage with a man named Lodovico to speak with Othello
  • Lodovico explains that Othello has been called back to Venice and that he should leave Cassio in charge
  • Othello is very rude and abrasive towards Lodovico, and even worse tempered towards Desdemona, he strikes her and accuses her of sleeping around
  • Lodovico is shocked by the Othello he is seeing, as he is used to a calm and collected Othello
  • Act 4 Scene 2
  • Othello and Emilia are alone on stage
  • Othello questions her about Desdemona and her alleged affair
  • Though Emilia denies any such thing, Othello has Desdemona summoned so the two can talk
  • Othello loudly chastises Desdemona about being unfaithful, his comments are unfounded and he embarrasses her greatly
  • Othello storms out after yelling at her leaving Emilia to comfort Desdemona
  • Emilia brings in Iago to sort the issues out, she questions him on why Othello would believe such untrue things about his wife
  • Iago assures them that Othello is acting this way because he is upset about official matters, however Iago is thrilled because his plan has worked exactly as he intended it to
  • Roderigo enters to speak with Iago, he is still upset about the failed attempt to court Desdemona
  • Iago tells him of a plan to still win her back
  • Iago lies and says Othello has been chosen to travel with his men to Mauritania in Africa, a lie, and that Desdemona will be travelling with him
  • Iago tells Roderigo that the only way to win over Desdemona is to get Cassio out of the way by killing him, Iago believes Roderigo is the man for this
  • Act 4 Scene 3
  • Othello leaves Desdemona to prepare for bed while he goes for a walk with Lodovico
  • Desdemona speaks with Emilia in her chamber and the two discuss infidelity and the circumstances under which it often occurs
  • Desdemona is saddened because she states that she would never do such a thing, and she is afraid that Othello will kill her for the accustions against her
  • The two remorse and prepare for Othello's arrival later that evening
  • Act 5 Scene 1
  • Roderigo and Iago wait outside the brothel where Cassio is visiting Bianca
  • Iago has prepared Roderigo with a rapier to which to stab Cassio with
  • Roderigo takes his opportunity and ambushes Cassio outside the brothel, however he fails to kill him, and is in turn stabbed himself
  • Iago enters and takes his chance and stabs Cassio then runs off
  • Othello overhears the commotion and comes to see Cassio has been stabbed
  • Othello believes that Iago must have done it and exacted his revenge for Othello
  • Othello heads off to prepare to kill Desdemona
  • Lodovico and Graziano hear the commotion as well, they enter however cannot see what has happened because of the darkness
  • Iago enters as well, however with a light
  • He finds Cassio and Roderigo injured on the ground
  • Iago pretends to be on Cassio's side and stabs and murders Roderigo for attacking Cassio
  • Eventually Lodovico and Graziano are able to see Iago and Cassio, the three men question Cassio as to what occurred despite the fact that Iago already knows
  • Bianca enters the stage and sees a wounded Cassio, she is frantic by what has happened
  • Cassio cannot explain what has happened as he does not even know who Roderigo even is
  • Roderigo's body is removed and Cassio is taken away by attendants
  • All has gone according to Iago's plan so far
  • Emilia enters the stage and speaks with Iago, Iago sends her to tell Desdemona and Othello what has happened
  • Act 5 Scene 2
  • Othello approaches the sleeping Desdemona and prepares to kill her, howevershe wakes while he's standing over her
  • Frantically she questions his motives to kill her and asks to be allowed to live
  • Othello tells her that he believes she has slept with Cassio and that Cassio even admitted it, however since Cassio is dead their can be no further investigation
  • Othello then begins to smother her
  • Outside Emilia cries out for Desdemona, and Othello, thinking it is Desdemona's cries continues to smother Desdemona
  • Believing her dead, Othello finally gives up on the smothering and lets Emilia in
  • Emilia claims that Cassio has killed Roderigo and that Cassio is in fact still alive
  • Othello is confused, and to add to this Desdemona cries out one last time before dying
  • Othello brags about killing her and tells Emilia that she must keep quiet, however she cries out bringing attention to Montano, Graziano, and Iago into the room
  • While the scene unfolds it is soon becoming common knowledge that Iago was behind all of the trickery, and that the affair was never true
  • Othello is visibly disturbed by this, however he cannot completely believe it and begrudgingly sticks by the belief that Desdemona was unfaithful
  • Graziano makes a statement and expresses relief that Brabanzio has recently died and therefore not outlived his daughter
  • Emilia then begins to mount evidence that Iago was a liar, causing Othello to charge and try to attack him, however he is stopped by Montano
  • This gives Iago a chance to stab and kill his wife Emilia and then flee, only to be pursued by Montano and Graziano
  • As Emilia lays dying she tells Othello that Desdemona was completely faithful to him
  • Othello arms himself while Montano and Graziano capture Iago, they then bring him back to stage along with Lodovico and Cassio who have joined the group
  • Othello stabs Iago yet does not fatally wound him
  • Othello is then disarmed and the people on stage discuss the events
  • Iago refuses to go into detail, yet a note is procured from Roderigo's pocket explaining what has occurred
  • Cassio then explains that he had found the handkerchief that cause all the suspicion in his chamber and he had no idea where it came from
  • The group tells Othello that he will be returned to Venice, stripped of power and put on trial
  • Othello tries to defend himself with all the good he has done in his time as a leader in Venice, however this does not help
  • He then says he will die on a kiss, and goes to Desdemona's dead body and kisses her, soon dying
  • Lodovico chastises Iago for what he has done, he then puts Iago in the care of Montano to be executed
  • Graziano is named as Othello's heir
  • Lodovico then returns to Venice to tell of what has happened
