Themes

  • The Conflict Between Military Heroism and Love
  • Othello is a dedicated and valuable soldier in Venice, he is also well respected
  • Being older and newly married, he has spent much of his life focused on being a military man as opposed a husband
  • Though he loves his wife Desdemona very much his military roles will always come first
  • This proves to be a problem as the play wears on however, as Othello is summoned to Cyprus on military matters and choses to bring Desdemona along with him
  • Othello had originally wooed Desdemona with his tales of military heroism as it gave him great confidence
  • However once in Cyprus with seemingly no military duties to take care of his confidence begins to wane
  • He fears of losing his wife which ultimately makes him vulnerable to Iago's deception
  • Iago is able to get under Othello's skin and convince him his wife is unfaithful
  • The powerful military man does not like to not have control in a situation, and the lack of control in his wife's alleged affair leads him to seemingly crack
  • He cannot separate the two lives from each other and therefore he is subject to downfall
  • The Danger of Isolation
  • As the play shifts from the large city of Venice to the smaller island town of Cyprus the plot thickens
  • The new setting is a very isolated as it is a small island with strong fortifications
  • On this island the main characters are in close proximity very often however they each desire their own individual isolation
  • Iago often speaks to the audience in soliloquies
  • Roderigo is only connected to Iago and therefore is isolated from the other characters
  • Othello seeks to be alone in contemplation at times as he contemplates his wife's alleged infidelity
  • Each character desires their isolation, yet due to the setting it is not so hard to come by
  • Therefore the unfavorable setting that isolates the characters from society, however not providing them with the isolation they needs is a recipe for disaster
