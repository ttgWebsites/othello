Characters

  • Othello
  • Othello is the protagonist of the novel, he is a very well respected man throughout Venice and is a powerful general
  • Othello is aging however and is quite insecure about his age and status, especially regarding his wife Desdemona as he is afraid the age difference will be too much for their relationship to work
  • His insecurity brings about his downfall
  • Desdemona
  • The young daughter of a Venetian senator Brabanzio
  • Desdemona falls in love and marries Othello secretly before the play begins
  • She is a loving and kind woman
  • Iago
  • Iago is a secret villain throughout the play
  • He pretends to be loyal to Othello however he spends the whole play plotting his and other characters downfall
  • Iago is also married to Emilia however he does not trust her after he believes she has slept with Othello
  • Michael Cassio
  • Cassio also works under Othello as a lieutenant
  • Cassio is very loyal to Othello unlike Iago
  • Iago despises Cassio and his role with Othello, and plots to turn them against each other
  • Emilia
  • She is married to Iago and also an attendant to Othello's wife Desdemona
  • Emilia is very close with Desdemona and meanwhile very distant from her husband as he does not treat her properly
  • Bianca
  • A prostitute in Cyprus who's main client is Cassius
  • She has feelings for Cassius, however he does not quite feel the same
  • Brabanzio
  • Desdemona's loving father, he is a senator in Venice
  • He dies before he can learn about the death of his daughter
  • Montano
  • The governor of Cyprus
  • Lodovico and Graziano
  • Two kinsmen of Othello, they both play minor roles in the play
